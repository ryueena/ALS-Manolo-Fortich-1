What is ALS - Alternative Learning System?

The Alternative Learning System in the Philippines is a parallel learning system designed for out-of-school youth and adults who haven't completed formal education.

It offers a chance for them to develop basic and functional literacy skills, access equivalent pathways to basic education, and ultimately improve their lives.

ALS includes both non-formal and informal sources of knowledge and skills, making it a flexible and accessible option.

Key Aspects of the ALS

ALS serves as a means for informal and busy students to achieve elementary and high school education without the need to attend daily classroom instructions.

Target:

Out-of-school youth and adults or OSYA who are unable to access or have dropped out of formal education.

Purpose:

To provide a "second chance" for OSYA to complete basic education, develop essential skills, and improve their quality of life.

Curriculum:

ALS has its own ALS K to 12 curriculum, which is streamlined but aligned with the formal K to 12 curriculum.

Delivery Methods:

ALS can be delivered through school-based and community-based programs, offering flexible learning options.

Components:

Basic Literacy Program or BLP: Focuses on literacy skills for reading, writing, and numeracy.

Continuing Education Program - Accreditation and Equivalency or CEP A&E: Provides an assessment of learner competencies, leading to a certificate equivalent to that received by graduates of the formal education system.

Implementation:

ALS program are implemented by DepEd-Delivered, DepEd-procured, and DepEd-partners including ALS teachers, district coordinators, and non-government organizations.

Benefits:

Access to Formal Education: ALS provides a pathway for OSYA to complete basic education and continue their studies.

Development of Skills: ALS helps individuals develop essential literacy and life skills, enhancing their employability and readiness for work.

Improved Quality of LifeL By gaining education and skills, participants can improve their economic and social well-being.
